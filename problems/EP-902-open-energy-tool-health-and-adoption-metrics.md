# Open Energy Tool Health and Adoption Metrics

## Problem

Track which open energy modelling tools exist, whether they are active, how
well documented they are, and where they fit in the wider open energy software
ecosystem.

## Why It Matters

Open energy work depends on more than the existence of source code. Tool choice
also depends on maintenance, documentation, package availability, community
activity, dependency health, and whether a tool is suitable for a particular
problem.

Tool-level metrics do not answer every problem-level question. They are useful
evidence for judging whether an open reference is alive, discoverable, and
maintainable.

## Scope

This problem tracks tool inventories, adoption metrics, repository metadata,
documentation links, and related open-source health signals for energy
modelling and planning software.

It does not treat repository activity as proof that a tool solves a specific
grid, market, forecasting, or flexibility problem.

## Existing Artefacts

Related contribution IDs are listed in front matter. Source URLs and licence
details require human verification before claims are upgraded.

## Open Questions

- Which tool-health metrics are useful for energy practitioners?
- How should tool-level metrics be linked to problem-level evidence?
- Which inventories cover German-grid, European-grid, and distribution-network
  tools well?
- How should the register distinguish a modelling framework from a concrete
  model of a particular grid or geography?

## Market-Integrity And Licence Notes

Tool metrics should be based on public repository, package, documentation, and
inventory data. Do not add private user data, confidential deployment details,
or unsupported claims about tool quality.

# Review Process

Reviews check factual support, licence clarity, reproducibility claims,
market-integrity risk, and taxonomy fit.

`review_status: needs_review` means the item is listed because it appears
relevant, but the register has not yet checked it to the standard needed for a
stronger claim. On the public site this is shown as `Review wanted`.

## Review Tracks

Community reviews can be small. A useful review may check one of these tracks:

1. Source review: the project, dataset, paper, or disclosure URL is the right
   source.
2. Licence review: code licence, data licence, reuse rights, and attribution
   requirements are clear.
3. Fit review: the item belongs under the stated problem and is not being
   overclaimed.
4. Evidence review: openness level and evidence strength are fair for the
   available artefacts.
5. Safety review: the card avoids live trading logic, sensitive data,
   operational instructions, and market-integrity risk.

## How To Contribute A Review

Open a review request issue or pull request with:

- the card ID, for example `isea-battery-revenue-index` or `EP-901`;
- the review track;
- the source checked;
- the conclusion;
- any caveats.

A review does not need to resolve every question. Partial reviews are useful if
they make one field more reliable.

## Maintainer Decisions

Status changes and evidence-strength changes require maintainer review. Public
commercial disclosures may be included, but must not be described as open
infrastructure unless the artefacts support that classification.

Maintainers may mark a card as reviewed only when source, licence, fit,
evidence, and safety caveats are clear enough for the stated scope.
